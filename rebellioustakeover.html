In the semi-cyberpunk, corporate-owned island city of Nexon, your only choice is to comply or fight back.
WE CHOSE TO FIGHT.

Rebellious Takeover is a hybrid-genre rush of fast-paced beat-em-up combat, heartfelt and comedic visual novel storytelling, and semi open-world exploration. Take on hordes of Veritas goons, reclaim the revolutionary C.O.R.E. technology they stole, and burn their empire to the ground.

It's time to take back YOUR future. Are you in?

Gameplay Breakdown

// What do you expect to see playing Rebellious Takeover?

High-Octane Combat

Engage in fast-paced, beat-em-up style combat. Chain together combos, master unique character abilities, and tag-team with your crew for devastating assist attacks to dismantle Veritas forces.

Slice-of-Life Story

When you're not fighting, hang out at the Subroutine hideout. Get to know the crew through visual novel-style interactions, build relationships, and unlock new missions, character upgrades, and crucial story moments.

Explore Nexon

Venture out into the sprawling, surveilled streets of Nexon. Discover side-stories, take on odd jobs for extra cash, and stumble into random encounters with Veritas goons and shady locals in a Yakuza-style freeroam environment.

Intelligence Database

// Leaked intel on the world of Nexon.

SUBJECT: "VERITAS INDUSTRIES" // STATUS: HOSTILE, CITY-WIDE CONTROL

Think of every tech giant, every data broker, and every private military contractor rolled into one monolithic entity. That's Veritas, right to the core. They own the infrastructure, they own the media, they bought out and control the government, and they effectively own the city of Nexon. Their public-facing goal is "progress," but their actual business is data, control, and the unethical pursuit of profit at any human cost. They see the citizens of Nexon not as people, but as assets to be monitored, manipulated, and monetized.

SUBJECT: "VITHIUM" // STATUS: UNSTABLE, HIGH-ENERGY RESOURCE

Vithium is the miracle mineral that powers Nexon. It generates megatons of electricity from a single shard, making it thousands of times more efficient than any power source that came before it. The science is still not fully understood, but Veritas controls the supply. Originally considered a worthless fusion of copper and zinc, its true potential was unlocked only a few decades ago. Some, like Deren, knew of its power and it's potential when combined with other things far earlier, using it for projects Veritas is only now trying to replicate... and weaponize.

SUBJECT: "SUBROUTINE" // STATUS: CONFIRMED REBEL HIDEOUT

On the surface, Subroutine is a second-hand tech and repair shop owned by Deren Kael. It's a place for off-the-books cheap repairs and jailbreaking corporate hardware. In reality, the shop is a front. The lower floors serve as a military-level underground base, and the central hub and living quarters for the rebels actively working against Veritas interests. It's their armory, their planning room, and the only place in Nexon they can truly call safe.

SUBJECT: "C.O.R.E." (CYBERNETIC ORGAN REPLACEMENT & ENHANCEMENT) // STATUS: STOLEN PROTOTYPE, HIGH-RISK

The technology that started the entire conflict. Originally designed by Deren Kael as a life-saving Vithium-powered heart replacement, the C.O.R.E. blueprints were stolen by Veritas. The corporation is now attempting to mass-produce a volatile, misguided version of the device. Their goal isn't to save lives, but to create cyborg slaves, turning citizens into living batteries to fuel their empire. Only Deren knows how to create a stable C.O.R.E.; Veritas's attempts are dangerously unstable and could kill the host with a single software payload. It was never made for public consumption - But Veritas intends to make it so and gain from it.

SUBJECT: "THE EXTINCTION EVENT" // STATUS: CLASSIFIED, HISTORICAL

Decades before the current timeline, a malicious governmental leader initiated a city-wide rampage to exterminate all "mixed species," including cat-human hybrids like Deren and Phenn. This brutal campaign is a deep scar on Nexon's history and the source of immense trauma for the few survivors. While the law was eventually lifted, the event shaped the world's prejudices and forced the remaining hybrids into hiding, becoming a forgotten footnote in the official history books controlled by Veritas.

F.A.Q.

If you're talking about the base game, No. The base game does not have any explicit content, and only hints and suggests things. If you're talking about the DLC, Yes. Rebellious Takeover is designed as a complete, M-rated story that can be played to its fullest without any gaps in the game's lore and campaign; However, an optional 'Definitive Story' DLC is available for players who want the original, uncompromising version of the narrative. It turns the original game from mature to adult, so be wary.

This Definitive Story restores two key things:

1. The deeper look into the team's relationships:
All the unfiltered passion and intimate scenes. These are explicit in nature.

2. The darker truths of Veritas' power:
An unflinching look at the story's heaviest themes, including the full, sensitive mentions of trauma, depression, and the psychological cost of the rebellion.

This game is for young adults and adults alike. The game itself is a mature setting and anyone under the age of 16 should not be playing even the base version of the game.

TL;DR: The base game doesn't contain any explicit content. There's a DLC, but it isn't just a porn update, it contains actually adult topics besides explicit content. This version is for players who want the story as the game was destined for from the start. No fade-to-black on the passion or the pain.

It's a hybrid-genre game that blends three core elements:
- Beat-'Em-Up Combat: Fast-paced, high-octane action.
- Visual Novel Storytelling: A deep, character-driven slice-of-life story where you build relationships.
- Semi Open-World Exploration: Discover side-stories and encounters in the city of Nexon.

The story follows a small group of rebels trying to take down the monolithic corporation, Veritas Industries. The main conflict kicks off when Veritas raids an old hideout belonging to the team's leader, Deren, and steals the blueprints to his revolutionary invention: The C.O.R.E, a Vithium-powered heart replacement. The team must fight to get the blueprints back before Veritas can turn the life-saving technology into a weapon for corporate control.

At its heart, the game explores themes of anti-corporatism, found family, and neurodiversity. Most of the main characters are on the autistic spectrum and are simply trying to live their lives to the best of their ability. The story focuses on how this group of outcasts finds connection and fights for a better future in a world that wants to exploit them.

Rebellious Takeover is being developed in Unreal Engine 5. You can check the planned system requirements on the download page!

The game is still in active development! The best way to stay updated is by following the official devblog. When it releases, it will be available on storefronts like Itch.io and GameJolt, with links on our download page.
