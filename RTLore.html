General Info

Project Overview

PROJECT: REBELLIOUS TAKEOVER

A new project intended to be a vast but dystopian story based on both pre-existing OCs and new OCs to give them a home and a purpose. Decisions have been made to make this a game in Unreal Engine 5. Originally, it was a way to create content based on these OCs in a streamlined manner as the creator moved away from Roblox and into professional programs like Blender and Unreal Engine.

The story follows a group of rebels in the corporate-owned city of Nexon. Their main motive is to take down Veritas Industries and attempt to bring back a sense of normalcy to the city.

Tactical Notes (Saved Memory)

  • Nicknames: Nicknames for characters are strictly used in high-heat moments, like in battle or in moments where it's easier to say. Characters do not use nicknames in casual moments.
  • Tone: The story remains simple and not overdrawn. Goober (and by extension, the wholesome vibe) is generally against excessive swearing.

Vithium: The Power Source

Brief explanation on Vithium: Vithium is a mineral that somehow generates power on its own—though no one fully understands how. It can produce megatons of electricity, making it thousands of times more effective than traditional generators. Though it was only officially discovered 20–30 years before the events of Rebellious Takeover , some - like Deren - knew of its existence far earlier. He even used it to create his heart replacement before it was categorized as a common mineral. At the time, Vithium was an obscure fusion of elements like copper and zinc, combined with a raw, chalky form previously thought to be as worthless as limestone - until the public figured out its true potential.

Subroutine is a second-hand tech and repair store (around the size of 4 corner shops, give or take) that Deren owns and does all of their work from. Lore/Backstory

Deren was born sometime around the time when Veritas was starting to grow as a company, but was not as all-powerful as they are now. Their father and mother figure are unknown - as they were killed in a rampage to kill off his species of cat-human hybrid due to a malicious governmental leader with intent to destroy all mix species.

Deren was left to live on their own in several towns and cities. During their early years, Deren was fascinated by technology - and it became a small hobby of his to invest his time into it. He often found himself interacting with technology stolen off of others like mobile phones, IPAs, media players and many other general electronic devices.

Eventually, after growing up enough to discover the rest of the world, Deren scavenged and searched around for a place to keep company in. They eventually found a village situated around the rainforests of the world, and set up there - As there were no governments or leaders to overthrow or destroy civilization of his species.

Being the last of his kind (or… so he thought), he delved deep into the world to try and find materials to create anything and everything he imagined. He started a very small company in the outskirts of the town he kept low in. He started making small devices to track pets, then small mobile devices better than the other big companies selling them - Until he decided to do his biggest project to date - Something that he called the C.O.R.E. allowing anyone the ability to achieve superhuman abilities through the power of Vithium, a niche mineral at the time.

Nobody knew how to use Vithium properly - except Deren through sheer luck and experimentation with the mineral. Deren was one of the first beings to be able to teleport with this technology, and was the first ever intelligent being to have their heart completely replaced and superseded by technology. Going forward, Deren was truly the first person to replace and truly supersede human organs and partially mechanise themselves. And from that point, Deren had one purpose for this. To use this powerful tool for good.

Key Changes

Similar in aspects to his previous iterations, still owns the C.O.R.E, Still uses Vithium in their resources, but a branch was hit early after their backstory where they purposefully do not have the actions of Derpz Fightin’ Grounds OR Revolture’s Trigger occur. Instead, a new thing happens - He relaxes for a couple years after the forced animal-human hybrid species extinction law was lifted.

Instead of Vithium being an activator for “Limitless Possibilities” on its own, It requires its own push through a serum that has only been found out by Deren that allows, under specific conditions, for god-like abilities. He doesn’t dare publicise this in fear of people using it for total destruction or enslavement of humanity.

Storyline & Motivation

Starting after his backstory, he lays back for a couple years. Eventually finds out he isn’t the last of his kind. Meets a girl called Phenn who has the same cat-human hybrid attributes as him. Eventually they become close.

Cue to probably close to 10 years after the events of his backstory. He’s in his 20s now. He owns a small tech shop in a cyberpunk-style city, Nexon. He does simple repairs and off-the-book jailbreaks and hacks for phones which have data-mining spyware on them from the ever-growing company, Veritas.

Soon enough, he finds out an old hideout that he used to have back when the extinction event for his species took place has been raided - by the same company he’s selling jailbreaks for. They found the blueprints to his old projects, but also ones which he refused to let the public know of due to its adverse instability. The C.O.R.E. The blueprints to the C.O.R.E were in the hands of a company so slimy that they were willing to actually mass-produce his creation. He finds this out from a television ad he sees that strictly shows a (very badly hurt) subject with a seemingly misguided and weird-looking version of his heart replacement technology.

After that, it’s not just about cracking the invasion of privacy on phones anymore. No. His motive changed. Get those damn blueprints back. It’s his. And he ain’t gonna let some slimy, greedy company get their hands on it just because they found it while he wasn’t there.

They could KILL PEOPLE if they mass-produced that and released it to the public. Control every aspect of their lives - There may even be zero-day software bugs that could render them useless, cause immense harm to the wielder, or worse - it could kill the host with a single payload in dramatic fashion. Only he knows how to create the C.O.R.E successfully. No greedy company ever will. So he bands up all he knows - Grabs friends of his, friends of Phenn’s, and slowly builds a small gang and sets up the upper floors of his shop as a hideout for them to plan and strategize revenge before they mass-produce it.

Lore

Mae always had a thing for creation and innovation. Even as a kid, she absolutely loved the concept of helping the public through technological advancements. Through sheer blood, sweat and tears, she was able to get a job at the biggest company in the world - Veritas - and finally get to make her genius ideas come to life. Or… so she thought.

Mae was once one of the elite minds behind Veritas Industries’ conceptual division, responsible for generating cutting-edge innovations, new technology pitches, and prototype ideation. Her job was intense but creatively fulfilling - until she saw what happened after her ideas left the boardroom.

Some of her seemingly harmless proposals - energy-based neural interfaces, wearable subconscious translators, emotion-mapping implants - were repurposed by Veritas into deeply unethical surveillance and control tools used to enslave the people of Nexon. Projects that began as medical or accessibility ideas were twisted into advertising and social manipulation tools solely for the company’s gain, not to actually help people in need. Through this, she realised there was no real way out of the corporate line. The stress consumed her.

She began to vanish for hours at a time, chasing numbness. Physical pain became her only anchor - Her only way to stop her from giving up entirely - proof she was still human in a machine that no longer needed her. She held onto one fading hope: that someday, somehow, she'd be free - free to build for people, not for profit. She knew they didn’t care. No one did. She and everyone else were just cogs - disposable the moment they cracked - And truthfully, she was getting to a point where she was actively deciding whether or not just to end it all just to get out of this corporate sludge that’s eating her whole or suffer just a little more in hopes that it gets better.

At one point on her daily shift, when she was mid-test of an incredibly dangerous contraption - A prototype of Deren’s C.O.R.E, which she soon realised wasn’t even Veritas’ creation at all - just stolen tech from a rogue genius working to help people, being used to turn people into living batteries - Wired-up, soulless husks - cyborg slaves fueling Veritas’ ever growing empire, she snapped witnessing a test subject beg for mercy, deciding she can’t take this anymore, and attempted to quit on the spot after realising how much blood is now on her hands.

Unfortunately, that was the worst mistake she ever made. People who quit at Veritas aren’t walked out - they're wiped out. Brutally. Quietly. Permanently. Security was closing in. Her ID chip was already flagged. She could hear the mechanical whir of a drone booting up behind her - counting down the seconds left in her existence... until Deren & his team - Originally on a mission to neutralise and shut down this first prototype of his creation - just so happened to witness as Mae, seconds before she was about to be decommissioned - Permanently.

She was seconds away from death - until the team swiftly recovered her and made sure she lived. She knew who they were (from one of her higher ups complaining about their team days prior) and realising that more importantly - Deren is alike to her in that they both create inventions for helping the public, not for corporal gain. After this, She now lives at Subroutine, owing her life to Deren - not just for pulling her out of the corporate hellhole, but for being there when the system had already decided she was obsolete.

Storyline & Motivation

Mae’s arc for her storyline and motivation is all about redemption. She knows the monsters Veritas unleashed came from minds like hers - if not herself specifically. That eats away at her self-worth daily, but instead of lashing out, she becomes the team’s sharpest mind for reverse-engineering Veritas tech, turning it back on the enemy with an almost surgical precision.

Her motivation is twofold. Make sure her ideas and the team’s ideas (Deren mainly) are never used to hurt people again, and Protect Luca, the one person who believed her even before the fall - And who’s now walking a razor’s edge to protect the team from inside Veritas.

Modes & Features

3 Distinct Gamemodes

    To unlock the missions, upgrades, character bios, and improve relationships within the team (for extra power when tagging them in mid-fight HiFi Rush style, like Lyra magically appearing and beaming a bunch of enemies around you with her sniper), you have conversations and stuff with protagonists while you're playing as them.

  • 3. Freeroam: Yakuza-style exploration of Nexon.

Freeroam

Exploration Mechanics

Yakuza Style

Then, there's the one we may add in the future - The freeroam yakuza style gamemode. Where you explore Nexon for rewards outside of campaigns and relationship novels.

The premise of this is from because of the fact that Nexon is surveillanced by Veritas so much, the outside turns into a Yakuza-style system Where you can interact with NPCs and stuff, but turn a wrong turn and you may enter combat with someone - Like Veritas goons, General bad guys (thiefs, thugs, gang members). Coming out of that alive will gain you extra points towards upgrades in the future.

Basically, when it comes around to free-roam, I wanted it to be primarily for you to fight people (as in, randomly spawning Veritas goons, Yakuza style), interact with NPCs around the map, interact with small shops and stuff around the map to get buffs (like Rumbling Ramen, the ramen shop nearby Subroutine that has the owner give the team a massive discount) or random sidequest encounters (Like a Veritas-created sweep bot in the parks of Nexon realizing they're apart of a horrible company and wanting to be "set free", and that's just codeword for "KILL ME".)

Hardcore Mechanics

And it might be the case that I might also add a hardcore mechanic where it acts like a roguelite, slowly increasing in difficulty the more you stay outside. Higher rewards, but higher risk. It might end up being that at the game's final few missions your game is also basically set onto a proper hardcore - One death and you're back to your last save.
